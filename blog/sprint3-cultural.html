IQ, EQ and their Differences

Reflections on Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence (EQ) is the ability to identify, recognise and manage your own emotions and actions, as well as those of others to produce positive outcomes. EQ requires an individual to differentiate between emotions, label them and conceptualise how they result in behaviours.

EQ can be understood in five areas:

  • Self-awareness
  • Self-regulation
  • Motivation
  • Empathy
  • Social skills

How does it differ from IQ?

In contrast to Emotional Intelligence, IQ is a metric used to measure a person’s cognitive ability. IQ is understood from a standardised test and relates solely to an individual’s intelligence.

Why is it important?

EQ is important because it essentially dictates every interaction a person will have with others (and also themselves). The ability to understand and regulate emotions will have a massive impact on a person’s social interactions and their ability to manage stressful situations and scenarios outside of their control.

Because of the nature of the world and life, high emotional intelligence can have huge benefits as we are often in varying circumstances that aren’t always positive. Our ability to manage ourselves in a way to remain calm and positive is immensely helpful when the going gets tough – this is something IQ cannot help with.
